**By Michael Vlamis //

_Note:_ The prototype was only accessible on the creator’s personal device, so I was unable to capture screenshots independently. 
Observations are based on direct interaction with the app during the evaluation session.

### Overview

This heuristic evaluation examines The Gallery, a prototype designed as a searchable library of reaction images for use in messaging. 
The app allows users to quickly find, copy, and upload reaction images, as well as organize them through a tagging system. 
It also includes a “Converse Mode,” which suggests reaction images based on live conversation input. 
The intended users are people who frequently use reaction images while texting or chatting online, a younger, more online audience.

The tasks I performed included: (1) searching for a reaction image, (2) uploading and interacting with images and tags, and (3) testing Converse Mode.

### Heuristics Used

- Visibility of system status
- Match between system and real world
- Recognition rather than recall
- Consistency and standards

---

### Usability Issues

**Issue 1:** Converse Mode interaction is unclear

_Severity:_ 3 (Major usability problem)
Heuristic(s): Visibility of system status, Match between system and real world

When switching to Converse Mode, it is not immediately clear that the user must press a large button (white circle) to activate it. 
I initially expected the feature to begin automatically upon entering the mode. 
This caused a moment of confusion before realizing an additional action was required.

Additionally, the way Converse Mode selects images is primarily based on matching keywords with tags, 
rather than understanding the overall context of the conversation.

_Impact:_
This creates a disconnect between user expectations and system behavior. 
Users may be unsure whether the feature is active, and the results may feel less relevant or intelligent than expected.

---

****Issue 2:**** Hidden press-and-hold interaction for editing tags

_Severity:_ 3 (Major usability problem)
Heuristic(s): Recognition rather than recall, Consistency and standards

Editing tags requires a press-and-hold gesture on an image. 
This interaction is not visually indicated and would likely be difficult to discover without prior explanation.

_Impact:_
Users may not realize that tag editing is possible at all. 
This limits the usability of an otherwise important feature for organizing and customizing the image library.

_Suggestion:_
Adding a visible edit icon or mode (similar to a settings button) would make this feature more discoverable. 
A small hint or message could also inform users that press-and-hold is an alternative interaction.

---

**Issue 3:** Tag system “catch-all” category could be clearer

_Severity:_ 2 (Minor usability problem)
Heuristic(s): Consistency and standards

The tagging system is generally well-organized (e.g., by format and emotion), 
but the general tag category acts as a broad catch-all (e.g., “art,” “dog,” “family guy”). 
While functional, this category could become inconsistent or harder to navigate as the library grows.

_Impact:_
As more images are added, inconsistent tagging could reduce the effectiveness of search and filtering.

---

### Conclusion

Overall, The Gallery is a highly functional and well-developed prototype, with strong core interactions and a clear concept. 
The issues identified are primarily related to clarity and discoverability rather than fundamental design problems.
