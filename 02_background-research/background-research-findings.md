# Background Research Findings

To inform the design of this project, I conducted background research in two areas: academic studies on friendships and social connection, 
and existing digital tools that support communication, planning, or relationship maintenance.

### Academic Research Insights

Research shows that friendships are crucial for mental and physical well-being. Supportive friendships correlate with higher life satisfaction, 
lower risk of depression, and improved emotional regulation. 
Even minimal interactions with friends can positively impact health, highlighting the importance of maintaining connections regularly (Abrams, 2023).

However, friendships require intentional effort. 
Young adults often struggle to sustain relationships due to busy schedules, misunderstandings, and competing responsibilities. 
Without regular engagement, relationships can weaken, and social isolation is increasingly common (Bruckmann, 2025).

These findings emphasize that maintaining friendships is not automatic—it requires planning, consistency, and small acts of connection. 
This justifies designing a tool that actively supports users in nurturing friendships, something that does more than just logistical coordination 
but also helps nurture ongoing meaningful engagement.

### Existing Tools 

I examined the current landscape of social apps, event planners, and relationship tools to understand how people currently maintain friendships digitally.

- Social / Communication apps like Instagram, Discord, and Messenger allow messaging and media sharing, but interactions are often fragmented and not focused on relationship maintenance. 
- Event planning tools such as Partiful, Facebook Events, and Google Calendar assist with scheduling and logistics, but prioritize efficiency over emotional connection. 
- Memory / photo-sharing apps (Google Photos, Snapchat, BeReal) document moments, yet do not actively tie memories to relationship growth or sustained engagement. 
- Relationship or habit-tracking apps (Clay, Monica, Habitica) offer reminders and tracking features but often feel transactional, geared more toward professional or networking contexts rather than genuine friendships. 
- Niche friendship apps like Locket and NoteIt explore intimate, low-pressure interactions for small groups, but their scope is limited and they do not support long-term friendship maintenance or holistic connection.

Across these categories, there is a clear gap: no existing tool fully integrates casual communication, shared experiences, memory, 
and reflection into a single, emotionally meaningful platform for maintaining friendships over time.

### Implications for my project

Combining insights from academic research and existing tools:

Friendships need intentionality, consistency, and low-pressure engagement. 
Current tools address pieces of friendship maintenance but fail to combine communication, planning, and reflection in a holistic way. 

This project can fill that gap by creating a platform that:

- Supports ongoing, meaningful interactions beyond just messaging or planning
- Encourages repeatable social rituals and small acts of connection 
- Integrates tools for memory, coordination, and reflection, creating a more complete experience of sustaining friendships

This confirms that there is both a real need and a design opportunity for a tool that helps users intentionally maintain friendships over time.
