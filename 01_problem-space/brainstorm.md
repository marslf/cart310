# Brainstorming: Project Ideas Exploration

##  Baking and Cooking / Recipe Sharing App

**Main idea:** a platform to share recipes (either links you find online or your own recipes), photos, and baking experience

**Why?** 
- I love to bake and do it a lot
- I use many different sites and cookbooks + my own family recipes and often forget where I found one specific recipe I want to remake
- Creative + fun + visual

**Pros:**
- strong personal connection
- potential for cool / interesting aestheticss
- lots of content possibilities

**Cons:**
- Very saturated space (instagram and pinterest already very common online spaces to share recipes)
- Risk of being very similar to existing apps / websites
- Not a very unique / distinct problem

## Reading App

**Main idea:** an app to track and share reading, thoughts / reflections, progress

**Why?**
- I read a lot
- I have tried a few different reading tracker apps and none fit my needs perfectly, a lot of bad ones

**Pros:**
- easy to research
- clear functionality
- I already have some personal experience using this type of app and I know what I (and others) don't like about them 

**Cons:**
- also very saturated (Goodreads, Storygraph, Fable, Libib, Bookmory, Bookly)
- again could feel repetitive / too similar to others / not distinct enough

## Hosting / Event Planning App 

**I am probably going to go with this one! (most distinct idea + most inspired feeling)**

**Main idea:** an app to simplify planning hangouts / events / outings with friends
(chats, polls, shared lists, common photo albums, etc.), instead of using multiple apps.

**Why?**
- I host often
- I experience the issue of having to contact friends over different apps/platforms to host one event (instagram, text messages, discord, messenger, etc.)
- Relatable / fun

**Pros:**
- clear problem and feature ideas
- easy access to users
- fun and creative
- if designed well, not overlt corporate

**Cons**
- Similar tools do exist already (facebook events, however pretty much none of my friends use it enough for that to be a good solution)
- Need to be distinct enough from formal event planning tools
